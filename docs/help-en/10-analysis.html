10 Analysis

Graphs, impact, gaps and problem diagnostics

Analysis views help users answer what depends on what, where coverage is missing, which C3 items are affected and why a service is not ready.

Analysis theory

A catalogue is useful only when it can explain consequences. Graphs, readiness diagnostics, gaps and overlaps turn static records into decisions.

Analytical views

ViewPurpose
Services GraphShows service-to-service and dependency relations.
Service detail graphFocuses on one service and its neighborhood.
C3 GraphShows C3 taxonomy relations and linked entities.
Impact AnalysisExplains what could break when a service, capability or C3 item changes.
Capability gaps and overlapsShows where services are missing or duplicated for capabilities.

How to diagnose a service problem

Open service detail, read readiness warnings, inspect ownership and offering evidence, then follow dependencies in the graph. If a warning is architecture-related, continue to capability mapping or C3 detail.

Common mistakes

Do not treat graph distance as business criticality. A nearby node can be low risk and a distant dependency can be critical. Always combine graph context with lifecycle, owner, SLA, decision records and dependency type.
