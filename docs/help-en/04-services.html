04 Services

Service catalogue, Service 360 and service editor

The service area is the core working space for service owners and catalogue administrators. It covers catalogue browsing, service list, service detail, graph view, editor, portfolio and new service intake.

Why the service catalogue matters

A service is the main governance object. It connects consumer value, ownership, lifecycle, offering evidence, support model, dependencies, capability mapping and governance decisions.

Important editor areas

•Service identity

Service ID, title, summary, portfolio, domain, audience and lifecycle state.

•Publish gate

Rules that prevent publication when required evidence is missing.

•Lifecycle rules

Controlled transitions such as draft, active, retiring and retired.

•Offering evidence

Consumer-facing service offering, requestability, channel and value statement.

•Relationships

Dependencies, replacements, related services and impact paths.

•C3 mapping

Links from the service to capabilities, C3 services, applications and data objects.

•Support model

Owner, reviewer, support contacts, SLA and operational links.

•Audience policies

Who may request or consume the service and under what constraints.

Readiness rules connected to a service

Readiness warnings usually come from missing owner, missing summary, incomplete C3 mapping, missing offering evidence, missing review date, unclear dependency classification or absent exception. Fix the source field rather than hiding the warning.

Typical scenarios

•Create a service

Use the new service wizard, add owner and purpose, then complete mappings and readiness evidence.

•Prepare for publication

Open readiness, resolve missing fields, request review and record the decision.

•Assess change impact

Open the service graph and dependencies before editing relationships or lifecycle.

•Retire a service

Check replacements, consumers, decisions and dependencies before moving to retired.

Common mistakes

Avoid using raw import evidence as the primary business description. Do not publish a service without owner, offering evidence and review context. Do not change dependencies without checking impact analysis.
