06 Governance

Operations cockpit, readiness, reviews and decisions

Governance turns service catalogue evidence into auditable decisions. It covers readiness gates, review queues, decision records, exceptions, owner load and risk signals.

Governance meaning

The application does not only store services. It helps decide whether a service is ready to publish, change, operate or retire. The governance layer preserves why a decision was made and what evidence existed at that time.

Readiness rules

•Owner missing

Every published or reviewed service needs a responsible owner.

•Offering evidence missing

Users must understand what can be requested or consumed.

•C3 mapping missing

Architecture and capability impact cannot be assessed.

•Dependency classification missing

Impact analysis cannot explain what breaks.

•Review date missing

Governance cannot maintain continual improvement cadence.

•Exception missing

Known unresolved gaps need an explicit decision.

Governance reviews and decisions

A reviewer can approve, reject, defer or record an exception. The decision should include scope, rationale, expiry or follow-up where applicable. This creates an audit trail rather than a hidden judgement in a field.

Typical scenarios

•Service blocked before publish

Open readiness, fix missing evidence, request review, then record the decision.

•Service approved with risk

Document the exception, owner, due date and mitigation path.

•Owner is overloaded

Use owner load to redistribute responsibility or prioritize reviews.

•Capability gap accepted temporarily

Record the accepted risk and review date.
